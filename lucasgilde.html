Wapenschild Sint-Lucasgilde Antwerpen
---------------------------------------------------------

Ik zag in een groep op facebook over heraldiek de volgende afbeelding verschijnen:

Dit is het wapenschild van de Sint-Lucasgilde van Antwerpen.(boek van Génard 1883)
Centraal staat het eigenlijke wapenschild, azuur drie wapenschilden van zilver,
met als schildhouder een gevleugelde stier. Deze is het symbool voor de evangelist Lucas.
Sint-Lucas is ook al eeuwenlang de patroonheilige van de kunstschilders.
Ralf Hartemink van de site Heraldry of the world, schrijft hierover: (Stock gillyflower = violier)

The most famous of the Painters' Corporations was that of Antwerp however; it could boast on the membership of people like Rubens and Jordaens. At the end of the Seventeenth century this guild displayed a very elaborate coat of arms showing, besides its own arms, those of the livery companies allied to it. The blazon reads: azure, three escutcheons argent, the shield encircled by a wreath of stock gillyflowers Or, stalked and leaved vert, (representing the Chamber of Rethoric called the Stock gillyflower) surmounted by a winged bull's head couped affronty Or, supporting between the horns a castle of three towers masoned sable, standing on a wreath Or and gules and with two lances proper emerging from behind the walls fixed thereto two banners gules, each charged with a hand couped appaume Or, the dexter a sinister and the sinister a dexter hand: issuant from behind the wings a ribbon gules, encircling the wreath of flowers and pendant therefrom eleven shields which are:
dexter
(1) azure, three covered cups Or for the goldsmiths,
(2) Or, an eagle displayed sable, beaked and membered gules, holding in its dexter talon an inking-pad and in its sinister a scroll argent for the printers,
(3) gules, on an embroidery-frame Or, an embroidery cotton argent charged with a dexter hand couped appaume gules, within a wreath of six roses, alternately gules and argent, leaved vert for the embroiderers,
(4) argent, a dexter hand couped appaumé gules, for the carpet weavers,
(5) gules a chest, Or, for the chestmakers,
sinister:
(1) azure, a pair of pincers argent, between in chief a hammer fessways lying on a lead-knife and a rod in saltire all Or and in base two glazing irons in saltiree surmounted with a tool consisting of a ball with two points emerging in fess Or, everything conjoined with a ribbon gules for the glaziers,
(2) gules, two tankards in chief and a jar in base argent for the potters,
(3) gules, the upper part of a broken sword Or for the gilders,
(4) gules, a button-chart argent, buttoned azure for the button-makers,
(5) gules, a dexter hand couped appaumé argent, issuant from a flint and steel reversed and reaching for a quatrefoil in chief Or for the papermakers,
Base: gules a dexter hand couped appaumé argent for the calico-printers,
the motto is: WT JONSTEN VERSAEMT (Gathered by affection).
